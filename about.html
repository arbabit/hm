Our Vision & Mission

Empowering underprivileged communities across Pakistan with dignity, integrity, and transparency.

Established 2024

Commitment to Empowerment

Founded on 29th November, 2024 under the vision of Mr. Muhammad Haroon Mahmood, a respected industrialist and philanthropist, the Haroon Mahmood Welfare Trust (HMWT) is committed to empowering underprivileged communities across Pakistan through private resources.

Since May 2025, HMWT has been actively engaged in livelihood support, healthcare, education, and community developmentâ€”ensuring compassion and accountability in every initiative.

Our Mission

To create sustainable impact through programs that promote:

  • Financial & livelihood support for vulnerable families.
  • Access to education and vocational training for youth and women.
  • Healthcare assistance for the poor and medically needy.
  • Community welfare aligned with corporate social responsibility (CSR) values.

Our Vision

A community where everyone has access to basic healthcare, education, and dignified living.

Our Values

  • Integrity: Honesty and strong moral principles in all our dealings.
  • Transparency: Open and clear communication in all operations.
  • Community First: Prioritizing the needs and well-being of the communities we serve.
  • Sustainability: Ensuring long-term positive impact through resilient programs.

Regional Impact

Between May and October 2025, HMWT supported over 170 households and institutions across Pakistan.

Governance & Financial Integrity

HMWT operates with strong governance, transparency, and Shariah compliance. All financial transactions are fully documented and audited. Disbursements follow a three-step review: Verification, Approval, and Audit.

Shariah Compliant Audited Documented EAD Compliant

The Way Forward

HMWT maintains a structured Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E) framework for transparency and impact tracking, utilizing a digital system for approvals and fund transfers, alongside field verification of beneficiaries and outcomes. Monthly and quarterly reviews by management and the Shariah Committee ensure continuous improvement.

The Trust aims to expand outreach to more regions, strengthen monitoring systems, and introduce sustainable livelihood models for lasting community resilience.
